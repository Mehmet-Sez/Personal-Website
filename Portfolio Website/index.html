HISTORY

How did I get to this point?

My first initial jump into the world of design actually started with Minecraft when I was 9 years old. Creating servers for friends to join and playing around with mods. Soon I found myself trying to understand Java, 3D modeling and studying game mechanics. This is also where I started to understand the concepts of marketing and how the presentation of a product is sometimes more important than the product itself. So I created a WIX account, logged on to multiple forums and started promoting my projects.

Fast forward 11 years and I had racked up a significant amount of experience in 3D modeling and game development/design through Unreal Engine. While studying Industrial Design at CCM (County College of Morris), these skills found themselves useful helping me create various product designs and colorful renderings. After obtaining my Associate’s, I transferred to TCNJ (The College of New Jersey), which is where I am now in the process of completing my Bachelor's in Interactive Multimedia.

Film is essentialy photography in motion. A step-up from static imagery. However film is not constrained to just visuals. Audio plays a significant role in story-telling. Conversations turn to music and footsteps turn to songs.

LET THE WATER RUN

SHORT THRILLER FILM

►

February, 2025
Panasonic fz300, Premiere Pro

“Let The Water Run” is a short form thriller film shot and composed over a 3 week period. Meant to invoke a feeling of raw realism, through a jarring ending showcasing the harsh realities of the world. Viewers are left shocked as they are thrown from an uncanny, uncomfortable space back into a very real location showcasing very real consequences. Blues, light magentas and desaturated greens were incorporated in the color grading to establish a cold, dark, dreamy atmosphere. Practical lights with dark shadows and raised highlights brought forth a depressive mood, utilizing tungsten light tones to add bits of warmth throughout the film. Cinematography involved a great deal of cycling through extreme angles, close-ups and wide shots to symbolise the character’s changing emotions and to leave the audience feel uncomfortable. The set design allowed for the uncanniness to leak through the screen as everything was created as minimally as possible in a claustrophobic way.

SCHNEIDER ELECTRIC GOOGLE AD

ANIMATED ADVERTISING DESIGN

July, 2025
After Effects, Illustrator, HTML/JavaScript

A Google Ad made for Schneider Electric showcasing one of their transfer switches. First put together in Illsutrator by compositing the images and graphics together while utilizing layouts that coincide with marketing techniques. Later exporting and importing into After Affects to generate the motion seen in the videos. This process was focused heavily on simplicity and easy readability. A swift entrance made by the transfer switches followed by a darkening of the surrounding area, highlighting the rooms used by Schneider. Then soon after the labels scale up into view and the leader lines are animated towards their respective rooms. Rewinding everything at the end allows for a seamless transition into a loop. The final result is then put through JavaScript and HTML allowing it to be uploaded as a Google Display Ad, ensuring high quality and a low file size.

TALK TO ME GOOSE

ANIMATED POSTER

►

April, 2025
After Effects, Illustrator, Photoshop

An animated poster of an illustrated F-14 Tomcat fighter plane flying in and out of frame. The objective was to generate a poster using motion graphics as a way to inform the audience of the aircraft's main functionality. Its variable-swept wing design. Traced out in illustrator using a real photograph of the plane, the 2D rendering was then thrown into Photoshop and later put into After Effects to be animated. Getting finalized in Premiere Pro with touch-ups and sound effects the poster was completed. There was a large emphasis on motion and the feeling of speed, achieved by clouds flying by and slight shaking of the plane.

LONESOME WATERS

CREATIVE 3D RENDER

January, 2025
Blender, Lightroom

A 3D render of a scene depicting a car parked in front of a boat house on the coast by the ocean. A man can be seen in front of the house, contemplating something. The composition and framing is symetrical with the car and house equal distance from each other. The light emitting from the headlights of the car serves as a leading line for the viewer's eye to travel in an "L" shape. Though the image seems heavy on the let side because of this lighting, the reflection of the house's lights on the water helps to balance out the image. These assets were taken from a free library utilizing a Blender plugin. Once everything was put together the lighting and sky along with adjustments to the materials, brought the render to life.
