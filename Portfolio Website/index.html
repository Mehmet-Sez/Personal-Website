FILM

LET THE WATER RUN

SHORT THRILLER FILM

Technical Information

Panaosnic fz300

Gimbal

SmallRig Tripod

Shot on a Panasonic fz300 and edited through Premiere Pro, “Let The Water Run” is a short form thriller film shot and composed over a 3 week period. Meant to invoke a feeling of raw realism, through a jarring ending showcasing the harsh realities of the world. Viewers are left shocked as they are thrown from an uncanny, uncomfortable space back into a very real location showcasing very real consequences. Blues, light magentas and desaturated greens were incorporated in the color grading to establish a cold, dark, dreamy atmosphere. Practical lights with dark shadows and raised highlights brought forth a depressive mood, utilizing tungsten light tones to add bits of warmth throughout the film. Cinematography involved a great deal of cycling through extreme angles, close-ups and wide shots to symbolise the character’s changing emotions and to leave the audience feel uncomfortable. The set design allowed for the uncanniness to leak through the screen as everything was created as minimally as possible in a claustrophobic way.

ABOUT

ARTIST BIOGRAPHY

Hi! I am Mehmet Sezer, a multimedia designer. Cinematic photography, film production, graphic illustration, UI/UX and other deliverables are what I specialize in.

PHILOSOPHY OF CREATION

An insight into my personal processes that I use for design.

Real world happenings serve as an influence for every creative endeavor. Film, photo, paint, and other forms of artistic media all embody a sense of realism, as nothing is truly created through the mind alone. Sensory inputs that one feels are articulated and combined to generate the masterpieces we see on a daily basis. No one can create a new color that doesn’t already exist. We simply blend the colors together to create new noise.
